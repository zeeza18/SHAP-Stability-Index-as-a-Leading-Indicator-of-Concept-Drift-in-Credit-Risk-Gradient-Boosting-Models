  "SHAP Stability Index as a Leading Indicator of Concept Drift in
   Credit Risk Gradient Boosting Models"

MANUSCRIPT SUMMARY
We propose the SHAP Stability Index (SSI), a label-free, model-agnostic metric
that measures week-to-week stability of the top-20 SHAP feature-importance
vectors to provide early warning of concept drift in production credit risk
models. Across three public benchmark datasets (IEEE-CIS Fraud Detection,
ULB Credit Card Fraud, and Give Me Some Credit) and three gradient boosting
architectures (XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost), SSI consistently leads AUC
degradation by a mean of 7.83 temporal windows (~55 calendar days) with
p < 0.001 and Pearson r = 0.88. We demonstrate that SSI cleanly separates
three drift regimes without threshold recalibration and that conventional
PSI never breaches its standard 0.25 warning threshold on the IEEE-CIS
dataset despite persistent model degradation — a blind spot SSI resolves.

FIT TO APPLIED INTELLIGENCE
This work is directly relevant to the journal's scope: it contributes a
novel explainability-based monitoring technique grounded in SHAP theory,
validated empirically on real-world financial data, and addresses the
practical gap between regulatory monitoring requirements (SR 11-7 / SS1/23)
and the limitations of distribution-based drift detectors.
